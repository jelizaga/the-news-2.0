Positive Fantasies About the Future Linked to Symptoms of Depression

posted January 31, 2016
  • Four studies published in Psychological Science indicates a correlation between positive fantasies as to one's future and depressive symptoms.
  • Researchers used daily diaries of participants and questionaires to measure symptoms of depression.
  • The first study included 88 college students as participants.
    • A correlation was found between positive fantasies about the future and lower their symptoms of depression in the present, but greater symptoms of depression recorded 6 months later.
  • The second study included 109 5th grade children as participants.
    • A correlation was found between positive fantasies about the future and lower their symptoms of depression in the present, but greater symptoms of depression recorded 7 months later.
  • The third study included 78 college students as participants.
    • A correlation was found between positive fantasies about the future and lower their symptoms of depression in the present, but greater symptoms of depression recorded 6 months later.
  • The fourth study included 148 college students.
    • A correlation was found between positive fantasies about the future and lower academic effort and lower grades, as well as greater symptoms of depression recorded 2 months later.
  • Inducing positive fantasies may indeed produce depressive symptoms by encouraging people to enjoy their success prematurely in their minds, thus lowering energy and effort. - Gabriele Oettingen, New York University
